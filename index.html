Every single quote came from our research subject — a homemaker with no formal design training, no title, no salary. Every single principle is taught in design school.

The argument this game just made:

The gap between "homemaker" and "designer" is not a gap of skill or intent — it is a gap of recognition. Produced by gender norms that coded the domestic sphere as trivial, economic systems that made unpaid labor invisible, and design institutions that built their identity by excluding her. She selects materials, designs for atmosphere, iterates on workflows, and thinks about her users. The only thing missing is the word "designer" on the door.
